It's Like Tinder...but for jobs?

Um...Tindeed?

Tindeed is simply a pun based off of two popular online companies. By combining the names of the dating app Tinder and the large job search website Indeed, we have come up with a not-so-unique way for a user to sift through job offers available online.


And yes, we know that sifting through jobs is a lot less exciting than swiping through potential eternal companions.


So How do I use it?

Tindeed is designed to work quite similarly to the Tinder App, available for smartphones. In that app, choosing to 'swipe left' or 'swipe right' on a user's picture shows the user's interest in that person's profile. Similarly, Tindeed allows a user to choose to 'like' a job, or 'dislike' it, by clicking on the respective right or left buttons.


When a person chooses one of these options, another job immediately populates and the user again chooses to like or dislike it.


But, wait! Tinder relies on 'matching' with someone who likes you too!

Yeah, yeah, we know. In an ideal world, we could make a full Tinder clone for job searching, complete with job applicant profiles, recruiter profiles, in-app messaging systems, etc. In reality, we only had a week or so to complete the website. And even then, we procrastinated until the last possible moment. So no, recruiters aren't somewhere swiping left or right on your applicant profile. Just choose yes or no, then apply to the ones you chose to 'like.' Stop being so picky.
